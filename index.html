Naleśniki


Naleśniki is a kind of pancake that is flattened, rolled up, and usually contains something inside, usually liquid. The Nalesńiki is a traditional Polish Dish, and is very infamously known to have a variation containing at least Nutella.


Naleśniki is actually known by another widely-known name, Pancake Crepes (or simply Crepes).

You can place any filling or topping of your choice. Aside from the traditional maple syrup and butter, ingredients used as such may even vary from tradition to tradition!

History of the Naleśniki

Pancakes are recognized nearly every place in the world, and they can vary in shape and form from location to location. In Poland, for instance, there is a variation that is flattened and rolled up, and sometimes includes something inside like a pudding, sauce, or other ingredients such as bananas. Naleśniki is the result. Pancakes have several variations in Poland, and this dish is just one of them. Americans also know what Crepes are, and Europe has had pancakes spread nearly as fastly as they were introduced.
